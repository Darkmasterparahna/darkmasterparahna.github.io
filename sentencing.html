YCJA Sentences

They YCJA sentencing process has many different outcomes and ends up looking complicated. It looks complicated as they take into account each individual persons circumstances and gives an appropriate sentence.

Example of the YCJA Justice Process

The YCJA sentencing process seems very long and confusing but thats only because they need to take into account the different circumstances for each person. However, it is not as complicated as it seems.

If I were to simplify this process, I would say that if a young person gets charged for a crime and is serious they will go to court. They will be unable to be charged for adult sentences however and will most likely recieve something like community service or counselling.

If they do not get charged for the crime, the young person will get a warning or will be given a meaningful program based on their crime like community volunteering or with mental health workers. Once they are done, they will not recieve a criminal record or anything like that. If they don't finish the program they will have to go to court and recieve a judging as mentioned earlier.

Example of the Criminal Code of Canada's Justice Process

This is the Criminal Code of Canada's Justice Process. It seems much more straightforward but it usually ends up with the person having to go to court. If they are deemed guilty by the court then they will usually face incarceration or jail time. Neither of which really improves the person's overall attitude towards recognizing what they did wrong.

Sentences are held often in the community and they are under strict supervision


Youth custody facility


Community supervision


Maximum length of sentence is 2-10 years.


May be given an adult sentence if they are 15-16.

Imprisonment or Jail. Can be imprisoned for life.


Fines or discharges for small offences like no drinking alcohol


Suspended sentence and probation. May not be given a sentence and remains on custody and can walk but is under supervision


Restitution, which is to pay back for the victim in the form of monetary compensation


Intermittent sentence where the sentence is not continuous. Which means you might server jail time, but not days in a row. You could have breaks in between.

As you can see the adult sentences are much more harsh in comparison to the YCJA sentences. Adult sentences offer little to no ways to learn from their mistake while the YCJA has that opportunity and the opportunity to do it while still interacting with the community.
