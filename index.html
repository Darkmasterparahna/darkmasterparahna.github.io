The YCJA

The YCJA is the criminal youth justice system. Its main principles are crime prevention, rehabitiltation, reintegration and giving meaningful consequences to crime.

Crime prevention. The YCJA helps prevent crime by addressing the things that make up a young person's behaviour. It holds the person accountable and there are many different sentencing options to deal with the full range of crime a youth can commit.

Rehabilitiation. The YCJA with the consequences it gives helps rehabilitate and help them reintegrate young people who commit crimes back into society.

The YCJA ensures the youth that committed the crime gets a meaningful consequence related to their crime. That will affect their future behaviors and actions.
