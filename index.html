The YCJA

Crime prevention. The YCJA helps prevent crime by addressing the things that make up a young person's behaviour. It holds the person accountable and there are many different sentencing options to deal with the full range of crime a youth can commit.

Rehabilitiation. The YCJA with the consequences it gives helps rehabilitate and help them reintegrate young people who commit crimes back into society.

The YCJA ensures the youth that committed the crime gets a meaningful consequence related to their crime. That will affect their future behaviors and actions.

The YCJA is the criminal youth justice system. Its main principles are crime prevention, rehabitiltation, reintegration and giving meaningful consequences to crime.

The YCJA applies to / deals with people from 12 to 18 years of age who have recently found themselves in trouble.


The YCJA allows for offenders to get meaningful consequences including community service.


For people between the ages of 12 and 14, it is not possible to get an adult sentence. Older than 14 depending on how bad the crime was you may be given an adult sentence.


The privacy of the people involved in the crime will be protected.


People who fall under this youth criminal justice act will also get no criminal record unless the crime is too severe.

This is known as the criminal code of Canada. It deals with adults or people older than 18.


The usual consequence for crimes committed is going to court.


This criminal code of Canada explains sentences / consequences adults can get which can include imprisonment or going to jail.


This code does not protect the offenders privacy and often the name of the person who committed the crime will be public.


While committing crime while over 18, you will have a criminal record for every crime you commit.
